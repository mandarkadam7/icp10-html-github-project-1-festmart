DIWALI

Diwali, also known as Deepawali, is a five-day-long festival celebrated by Hindus, Jains, Sikhs and some Buddhists

   

DIWALI :-

This festival, also known as the festival of lights, is an Indian festival celebrated every year in the fall. On this day, people illuminate their homes and shops with diyas (lamps) and candles to celebrate the triumph of light over darkness.

CELEBRATION :-

It's one of the biggest festivals in India and is also celebrated in other countries with large South Asian populations, such as Nepal, Malaysia, and Fiji. Diwali is celebrated between mid-September and mid-November, and the date changes every year. The festival symbolizes the victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance.

FOOD :-

Diwali is a Hindu festival of lights that's celebrated with a variety of foods, including sweets, savory snacks, and more: Sweets These symbolize the sweetness of life and the joy of togetherness. Popular sweets include: Gulab jamun: A traditional dessert of deep-fried donut holes soaked in rose syrup Kheer: A pudding made with rice or vermicelli, and often includes sugar, jaggery, dates, saffron, and dry fruits Besan ladoo: A soft, deep golden-colored ladoo made with spiced gram flour Mysore pak: A soft, rich dessert from the city of Mysuru that's popular to exchange during Diwali Coconut ladoo: A traditional sweet that can be prepared at home in about 10 minutes Savory snacks These symbolize the victory of light over darkness. Popular snacks include: Samosas: Small, triangular pastry pockets stuffed with potatoes and peas, and flavored with spices like cumin seeds, coriander powder, and garam masala Pakoras: A fried snack Chivda: A fried snack.
